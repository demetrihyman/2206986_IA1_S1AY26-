Twelve Pillars

Chapter 4: Achieve Your Goals

Summary

In this Chapter, Michael meets Charlie at Mack's Diner. Michael shares that his relationship with his wife, Amy, is improving, which he attributes to the garden metaphor Charlie taught him. However, Michael admits he feels stuck in his career and isn't living up to his potential. Charlie asks him about his goals and Michael confesses he doesnt have any. Charlie then shares Mr.Davis's wisdom on goal setting. They key points include the power of writing down your goals to bring them into reality, and the importance of setting long-range, mid-range and short range goals. Most imprtantly, Charlie reveals Mr.Davis's core philosophy on goals, The major reason for setting a goal is for what it makes of you to accomplish it.The true value isn't the destination, but the person you become on the journey to get there.

Reflection

Sometimes as people we are caught up on the end product that we want to achieve, whether it's specific job, income, or possession. I have never thought of using a goal as a vehicle to my own personal development. But now I am asking myself Who do i need to become to achieve my goal? and because of this question I intend to write down my goals, not as some magical wishlist but as a roadmap for the person I want to become.

Chapter 5: The Proper Use of Time

Summary

Michael hates that he doesn't have the time to work towards his goals because he is swamped with work and family. Charlie counters with another of Mr.Davis's profound insights:We must all suffer from one of two pains: The pain of discipline or the pain of regret. The difference is discipline wighs ounces while regret weighs tons. Charlie explains that days are expensive you only have a limnited number. The key to using them wisely is to focus on what is important, not just what is urgent. If you dont design your own life plan, you will fall into someone else's plan, and their plan for you probably isn't very ambitious. The chapter concludes with the powerful idea that you either run the day or the day runs you.

Reflection

Firstly, I'd like to say I dont think I am using my time properly, but I try to. The small, daily effort of reading instead of watching youtube sometimes feels like a burden in the moment. But to look back on my life and see all thime times I squandered my potential is a pain measured in tons. Sometimes I legit small tasks consume my whole day leaving important tasks the bare minimum. One of my goals is master my time and not watch it pass me by while I do nothing.

Chapter 6: Surround yourself with the best people

Summary

Michael visits Charlie at the estate and Charlie then introduces the next pillar:Surround yourself with the best. This isn't about buying expensive things, but about choosing the right people. Charlie challenges Michael to analyze his three closest friends, questioning what effect they have on him, where they have him going, and most importantly , what they have him becoming . He introduces the concept of categorizing relationships into disassociation, limited association and expanded association. Michael struggles with the idead of leaving behind lifelong friends, but Charlie explains that to attract attractive, powerful, and committed people, you must first become attractive, powerful, and committed yourself. The people you surround yourself with have a profound impact on your attitude and your destiny.

Reflection

Honestly, it is difficult to evaluate the people around myself because sometimes I feel like the bad influence is me. But never the less, I will use this chapter not as a tool to discard people but as tool to make sure the people I surround myself with are people that inspire me to be better.

Chapter 7: Be a Life Long Learner

Summary

Charlie equate the last lesson to the next pillar, which says a successful person is always a life long learner. Then he shares a quote from Mr. Davis, Formal education will make you a living. Self-education will make you a fortune. Michael is a skeptical person especially about reading books, which he dismisses as a scam and too expensive. Charlie argues that one of the greatest learning tools are books, allowing you to sit with great thinkers and learn from them. Then to solidify his point he quoted Mr.Davis It isn't what the book costs. it's what it will cost you if you don't read it. To drive home the point Charlie gave Michael a copy of The Magic of Thinking Big and tells him he must read it before they meet next. This chapter also shows a moment where Michael notices Charlie's weight loss and walks slower, deepening their bond.

Reflection

This chapter is something I would say I already put in play in my life. Information is vast and easily accessible in this era so not absorbing as much as you can would be stupid. For this very assignment I have gone out of my way to find videos, and websites to help me this website the best I can right now.
